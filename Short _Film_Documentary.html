About the Film

A Tale of
Two Worlds

Energy poverty describes how a lack of access to electricity and sustainable energy sources leads to lower standards of living — and, ultimately, to poverty. It is a condition that shapes the lives of hundreds of millions worldwide, yet remains largely invisible to those who have never experienced it.

As an immigrant to Canada, filmmaker Samuel Iwelu has lived on both sides of this divide — spending his childhood in an energy-poor country and his adult life in an energy-rich one. This documentary does not observe from a distance. It contrasts those two lived realities with unflinching honesty.

"I wanted to show what it actually feels like when the infrastructure of modern life simply isn't there — and what it means when it suddenly is."

Beyond the personal story, the film explores the paths we can take to reduce energy poverty globally — from policy shifts to grassroots innovation — asking what responsibility we hold as individuals and as societies.
